About Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert light energy into chemical energy. The process involves:

  • Light Energy: Captured by chlorophyll in plant cells
  • Carbon Dioxide: Taken from the air
  • Water: Absorbed through the roots
  • Products: Glucose and oxygen

Key Concepts:

  • Light Energy: The primary source of energy for photosynthesis
  • Water: Provides hydrogen atoms needed for the process
  • Carbon Dioxide: Source of carbon atoms for glucose production
  • Oxygen: Released as a byproduct
  • Glucose: The main product used for plant growth

6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
