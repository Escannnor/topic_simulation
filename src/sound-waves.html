About Sound Waves

Sound waves are longitudinal waves that travel through a medium, such as air or water. They are created by vibrating objects and can be characterized by their:

  • Frequency: The number of complete waves that pass a point per second (measured in Hertz)
  • Amplitude: The height of the wave, which determines the volume
  • Waveform: The shape of the wave, which affects the timbre of the sound

Key Concepts:

  • Frequency: Number of waves per second (measured in Hertz)
  • Amplitude: Height of the wave (determines volume)
  • Wavelength: Distance between wave peaks
  • Wave Interference: How waves interact when they meet
