About the Water Cycle

The water cycle describes how water moves through Earth's atmosphere, oceans, and land. It's a continuous process that sustains life on our planet.

Key Concepts:

  • Evaporation: Water turning into vapor
  • Condensation: Vapor forming clouds
  • Precipitation: Water falling from clouds
  • Collection: Water gathering in bodies of water

Stages of the Water Cycle:

  • Evaporation: Sun heats water into vapor
  • Condensation: Vapor cools into clouds
  • Precipitation: Water falls as rain/snow
  • Runoff: Water flows over land
  • Infiltration: Water seeps into ground
  • Collection: Water gathers in oceans/lakes
